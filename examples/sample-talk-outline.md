# Sample Academic Talk Outline

This sample shows how the skill should structure a 15-minute social-science conference talk.

1. `A01_TITLE` — Paper title, presenter, department, university.
2. `A03_RESEARCH_PUZZLE` — The empirical or theoretical tension motivating the paper.
3. `A04_LITERATURE_GAP` — What prior work explains and what remains unresolved.
4. `A05_THEORY_FRAMEWORK` — The mechanism or normative framework.
5. `A07_DATA_DESIGN` — Dataset, sample, treatment/comparison, outcome.
6. `A08_MEASUREMENT` — Key variables and coding choices.
7. `A09_METHOD_MODEL` — Model, identification logic, and inferential assumptions.
8. `A10_MAIN_RESULT_FIGURE` — Main result as a figure with one-sentence interpretation.
9. `A11_INTERACTION_OR_HETEROGENEITY` — Conditional pattern or group difference.
10. `A12_MECHANISM_RESULT` — Evidence about mechanism.
11. `A14_ROBUSTNESS` — What checks preserve or qualify the result.
12. `A16_CONTRIBUTIONS` — Theoretical, empirical, and methodological contributions.
13. `A17_DISCUSSION_QA` — Focused discussion questions or Q&A.

The same structure can be expanded for a 30–45 minute workshop by adding section dividers, a fuller method slide, more mechanism evidence, and backup material.
