# Academic PPT Quality Checklist

## P0 errors — must fix before delivery

A slide lacks a clear argumentative function. A result slide uses raw software output without guiding the audience to the substantive finding. An interaction is reported only as a coefficient without predicted values, marginal effects, or another interpretable display. A table, figure, axis label, or note is too small to read in slide-show mode. The deck uses decorative gradients, generic AI-style icons, UI cards, or product-launch tropes that undermine scholarly credibility. An institutional crest is stretched, recolored, used as wallpaper, or repeated as decoration. A causal or empirical claim appears without enough context to evaluate sample, design, model, or uncertainty.

## P1 errors — fix unless there is a deliberate reason

Titles are labels rather than claims. Literature slides are citation inventories rather than conceptual positioning. The theoretical mechanism is described in prose but not visually organized. The data/design slide does not reveal the key comparison. Robustness is presented as a long checklist without prioritization. The slide count is too low for the density of the argument. Multiple findings compete on one slide. Color is used categorically without explanation.

## P2 refinements — improve polish

Align title starts and body grids across slides. Use one recurring location for model notes. Keep the crest and page metadata in consistent positions. Replace long bullets with short claims. Use direct labels in figures when possible. Ensure section titles are parallel in grammar and scope. Move highly technical material to backup.

## Final review questions

Can a listener reconstruct the paper’s research question after the first three slides? Can they explain the identification or comparison after the design slide? Can they state the main finding from the result headline alone? Does every chart show uncertainty or justify why uncertainty is not shown? Does the conclusion say what the paper changes in the literature rather than merely restating the topic?
